Fix Your Finances

Post by Cole Gendelman, July 26, 2019.
Wealth

Managing my finances have always been a struggle for me. From forgetting about subscriptions to overspending, I haven’t found a way to properly keep track of all of my expenses and review which categories I spend the most in. A friend of mine recommended this mobile app call Mint. I am sure many of you have heard of this app before but this was my first time. I am not kidding when I say that my finances were set up perfectly in only a few weeks after getting it. The perpetual cycle of bad financial management was solved with one app. It connects with all of my banks, continuously analyzes your expenses, and gives you spending and credit recommendations. This app has been great for me and I hope it can help you in the same way.

The Rise of Podcasts

Post by Cole Gendelman, July 25, 2019.
Self Wealth

Companies like Spotify, Apple Music, and Pandora used to be known solely as music streaming platforms. Now, they are considered media streaming platforms. This is due to the rise of Podcasts.

From sports analysis to true crime stories, podcasts offer a convenient way to get information.Companies like Spotify, Apple Music, and Pandora used to be known solely as music streaming platforms. Now, they are considered media streaming platforms. This is due to the rise of Podcasts. From sports analysis to true crime stories, podcasts offer a convenient way to get information. Here are a few podcasts that I like and a short justification on why you should listen.

  • How I Built This with Guy Raz - Guy Raz is an amazing journalist that interviews the founders of our favorite companies. This podcast is truly amazing in how it makes the founder of some multi-million dollar company seem more relatable. With each episode, you get a great story and a lot of applicable advice.
  • Sleep With Me - The Sleep With Me podcast is a creative approach to helping you sleep. Drew (the commentator) lulls you to sleep with his soft voice and incomprehensible rants. It was honestly hilarious the first time I heard it but after a few times It was really helpful in getting me to bed.
  • Side Hustle School - Chris Guillebeau is a brilliant mind that shares stories of people’s success in their side hustle’s. Each episode is about 7-11 minutes and is a perfect dose of inspiration for anyone looking to diversify their time. It is extremely easy to listen to and has helped me personally with some of my projects.

Collect your Thoughts with Jour

Post by Cole Gendelman, July 24, 2019.
Self

Whether it is my lack of focus or my fear of realizing how little I got done that day, journaling has always been a difficult habit for me to get into. I have read about its benefits and how it clarifies thoughts, resolves internal issues and reduces stress. Even knowing these things, I couldn’t get myself to do it. Until one day, I was scrolling through TechCrunch and saw an article titled Throw away your diary, Jour is a new app for guided journaling”. It captured my interest and, therefore, my attention for 15 seconds. The article checked two major boxes I didn’t know I had: guidance, and security. I realized that the issue’s I was having with journaling was partially due to the lack of instruction. Nobody was prompting me when to reflect or what to say. This is something people usually don’t want but it was necessary for this situation. Jour, not only reminds me to write every day/night at the time I scheduled but asks me questions that get me started. I have been using Jour for 3 months and I can honestly say it has improved my quality of life. I am sure there are other apps like this but Jour is my app of choice for now.

So, there many different types of languages and choosing one feels like a big responsibility. I promise you it is not. Once you learn how to code in one language, you develop code logic skills that make it MUCH easier to transition to other languages.

Here a link with descriptions of each language and reasons why you would learn each.
